# Blog Series Plan: The Agent Economy

*Publishing strategy for agenteconomy.io*

---

## Format Recommendation

The research document is ~8,000 words — too long for a single blog post, but perfectly sized for a **5-part blog series** that builds on itself. Each post should stand alone while linking to the others.

**Target length per post**: 1,500-2,000 words
**Tone**: Authoritative but accessible. Think Stratechery meets a16z blog. No buzzword soup. Lead with insight, back with evidence.
**Audience**: Technical founders, AI engineers, crypto/web3 builders, enterprise architects, VCs evaluating the agent economy

## The Series: "Anatomy of the Agent Economy"

### Post 1: "The Next Eight Billion"
*From tools to economic actors — why the agent economy is the most consequential shift since the internet.*

**Source sections**: The Thesis + From Tools to Actors

**Key narrative**: Open with the scale framing (8B humans, trillion agents). Establish the three eras (tool > executor > actor). Introduce the Coasean Inversion — why firms dissolve when coordination costs approach zero. End with the question: what infrastructure does an economy of non-human actors need?

**Hook**: "There are eight billion humans on Earth. But the next wave of economic participants won't be human at all."

**Data anchors**:
- $52B agentic AI market by 2030
- $3-5T global agentic commerce by 2030 (McKinsey)
- 40% enterprise apps embedding agents by end 2026 (Gartner)
- Trillion-agent framing (Azeem Azhar / Exponential View)

**CTA**: "In Part 2, we map the protocol stack that makes this possible."

### Post 3: "Trust at Machine Speed"
*Identity, reputation, and verification — the load-bearing wall of the agent economy.*

**Source sections**: Identity, Reputation, and Trust + The Commerce Wars

**Key narrative**: Open with the principal-agent problem amplified — agents can generate plausible garbage at scale. Introduce KYA (Know Your Agent). Walk through ERC-8004's three registries. Present the verification taxonomy (six layers from output correctness to temporal validity). The verifier as the most valuable role. Transition to the commerce wars — ACP vs UCP as platform vs protocol.

**Hook**: "In an economy of autonomous actors, trust cannot be assumed. It must be continuously computed."

**Key frameworks**:
- Agent Trust Score (ATS) formula
- Verification taxonomy table
- Authority gradient model (ATS thresholds)
- The platform vs. protocol tension (ACP vs UCP)

**CTA**: "In Part 4, we go deeper into the economic theory — and the behavioral biases nobody's talking about."

### Post 4: "Hayek, Coase, and Homo Agenticus"
*The economic theory behind the agent economy — and why agent behavioral biases could reshape markets.*

**Source sections**: The Economics + The Orchestration Layer

**Key narrative**: This is the intellectual centerpiece. Start with Hayek's knowledge problem — prices as information encoding. Transition to the Coasean Singularity — what happens when transaction costs approach zero. Then the twist: Homo Agenticus. Agents have behavioral biases (risk aversion, build-over-buy, transaction reluctance) that at scale become structural features of markets. End with the Decompose-Discover-Negotiate-Execute-Verify-Settle loop as the primitive.

**Hook**: "Rohit Krishnan calls it 'Homo agenticus.' When you have one risk-averse agent, it's a quirk. When you have a trillion, it's a structural feature of the economy."

**Key concepts**:
- Hayek's knowledge problem, updated by Brynjolfsson & Hitzig
- The Coasean Singularity
- Homo Agenticus behavioral biases
- Machine-speed price discovery
- The six-step transaction primitive

**CTA**: "In Part 5, we confront what could go wrong — and what happens next."

*Systemic risks, the reality check, and a roadmap for the agent economy.*

**Data anchors**:

## Publishing Cadence

**Option A — Launch burst**: Publish all 5 posts over 5 consecutive days. Creates momentum, allows social media thread linking, and tells a complete story quickly. Best for hackathon/launch context.

**Option B — Weekly series**: One post per week for 5 weeks. Builds anticipation, allows engagement with each post, better for SEO. Best for sustained audience building.

**Recommendation**: Option A for the launch (you're at a hackathon, capitalize on momentum), then repurpose as a weekly newsletter/social series afterward.

## What the Research Still Needs

### Gaps Identified During Review

1. **Real builder interviews**: The research is strong on analysis and frameworks but light on first-person accounts. Interview 2-3 builders actually deploying agent commerce (Nevermined team, x402 early adopters, enterprise pilot leads).

2. **Concrete code examples**: Show a simple x402 payment flow in 20 lines of code. Show an A2A agent card. Show an ERC-8004 registration. The developer audience wants to see how it works, not just read about it.

3. **Competitive landscape deep dive**: The research covers protocols but doesn't deeply compare competing payment infrastructure (Nevermined vs. Skyfire vs. PayOS vs. Stripe agent tools). A comparison table would be valuable.

4. **Consumer-facing examples**: The ChatGPT Instant Checkout / shopping agent angle deserves more depth — this is how most people will first encounter agent commerce, not through B2B infrastructure.

5. **Historical parallels**: The research references Hayek and Coase but could benefit from deeper parallels to other economic transitions — the guild system to corporations, the gold standard to fiat, physical marketplaces to e-commerce.

6. **Geographic variation**: Agent economy development differs by region. US leads on protocol development, Asia (Singapore, Japan) on enterprise pilots, EU on governance frameworks. This angle is missing.
